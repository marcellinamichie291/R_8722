2
The FoolUpright: Innocence, spontaneity, free spirit, potential.

Reversed: Naivety, foolishness, risk-taking.		EndbringersThey're children, they're unpredictable, and only at the end do they unleash their full potential. Their flaw is the fact that they're fundamentally hollow, naive, illusory. They're only really playing a game for reasons that don't matter at the heart of things.
3
The MagicianUpright: Power, skill, concentration, action

Reversed: Manipulation, poor planning, latent talents		ArmsmasterHe's driven, authoritative, trained, and active. This much is obvious, but his crisis and challenge are his lack of true power and his tunnel vision. His attempt at manipulating things is what breaks him.
4
The High PriestessUpright: Intuition, higher powers, mystery, subconscious

Reversed: Hidden agendas, need help to listen to inner voice		TattletaleTattletale is intuition, this is obvious enough, and her power remains a mystery to others for much of the story. But the crux of things, her failure, her struggle, is that she didn't recognize her brother's intent to kill himself, nor does she have the ability to hold back and listen to conscience or reason when pressed.
5
The EmpressUpright: Fertility, femininity, beauty, nature

Reversed: Creative block, dependence on others		Doctor MotherShe stands at the crix of the upright & reversed cards. A perverted sort of creativity, nature, and fertility, in the creation of altered life.
6
The EmperorUpright: Authority, father-figure, structure, foundation.

Reversed: Domination, excessive control, rigidity, inflexibility		CoilHe seeks to take over the city, seeping into every aspect of it and taking the power for himself. But his desire for absolute control is what breaks him in the end.
7
The HierophantUpright: Religion, group identification, tradition, beliefs

Reversed: Restriction, challenging the status quo		EidolonFaith, conviction, membership in the triumvirate and his understandings of his power defined him, but his hands were tied and his abilities limited. His crisis was in having to challenge the static, paralyzing understandings he had and, though doing so freed him, one final understanding destroyed him.
8
The LoversUpright: Union, love, relationships, values alignment, choices

Reversed: Disharmony, imbalance, misalignment of values		GrueGrue's central challenge is one of intimacy & attachment. He's driven by group affiliation and a need to attach himself to others, but disharmony and imbalance plague him at all stages of his life, first through Aisha, then through trauma.
9
The ChariotUpright: Control, willpower, victory, assertion, determination

Reversed: Lack of control and direction, aggression		ChevalierHe overcame his rage and his quest for vengeance as a youth and became a hero, perhaps the one most definitive of determination, willpower and strength.
10
StrengthUpright: Strength, courage, patience, control, compassion

Reversed: Weakness, self-doubt, lack of discipline		GolemA two year wait - patience indeed. To kill a monster, though he has no confidence - courage. He's a character raised by the hateful who became caring regardless. His crisis is one of an inability to find power, self-doubt (before and after he has powers) and a lack of training.
11
The HermitUpright: Soul searching, introspection, being alone, inner guidance

Reversed: Isolation, loneliness, withdrawal		ImpImp's personal challenge is one of being isolated, attempting to gain some agency when the rest of the world or her power are trying to take it away, separating her from the world. Even when working with the team, she's functionally alone.
12
Wheel of FortuneUpright: Good luck, karma, life cycles, destiny, a turning point

Reversed: Bad luck, negative external forces, out of control		Dinah AlcottThe oracle of Worm, who guided the hand of destiny, her crisis is entirely out of her hands, entirely out of her control- she has no blame in it, and relies on others to save her.
13
JusticeUpright: Justice, fairness, truth, cause-and-effect, law

Reversed: Unfairness, lack of accountability, dishonesty		AlexandriaShe's director of the PRT and head of the protectorate. But she commits wrongs and perpetrates a flawed system behind the scenes
14
The Hanged ManUpright: suspension, restriction, letting go, sacrifice

Reversed: Martyrdom, indecision, delay		DragonDragon is suspended, restricted, and her only ability to take true, independent, meaningful action is perpetually done at a cost of having to take a leap of faith, one that inevitably has a cost. She pays this price again and again, and holds herself back repeatedly.
15
DeathUpright: Endings, beginnings, change, transformation, transition

Reversed: Resistance to change, unable to move on.		TaylorTaylor transforms several times over the series, her crisis is one of identity, ever changing, and she only finds it at the end. Her inflexibility and the shadows of past stages of her life follow her, hampering her progress.
16
TemperanceUpright: Balance, moderation, patience, purpose

Reversed: Imbalance, excess, lack of long-term vision		LungLung stands at the crossroads of the Temperance ideals - he's patient, gathering strength both to defeat his enemies while he works in Brockton Bay, and later to play a role in things at a later date. But his recklessness and an excess of power lead to Kyushu being destroyed and his incarceration at two separate points.
17
The DevilUpright: Bondage, addiction, sexualism, materialism

Reversed: Detachment, breaking free, power reclaimed		RegentThe member of the Undersiders who is driven by a desire to seek pleasure and excitement, by material greed and a general need to -feel-, when the ability to do so is limited. Breaking free of his father's shadow is his personal crisis, and he's driven by a need to surpass his dad.
18
The TowerUpright: Disaster, upheaval, sudden change, revelation

Reversed: Avoiding disaster, fear of change		Jack SlashAll of these traits are key - he stands at the crux, as is appropriate; avoiding disaster is his crisis, in a weird way, because he's driven to escalate and challenge himself by the fact that his power keeps offering him a way to defeat his enemies.
19
The StarUpright: Hope, spirituality, renewal, serenity

Reversed: Lack of faith, despair, discouragement		PanaceaPanacea is hope, but for others, not herself. She is renewal, spirituality, and serenity when she finally does find herself, reinventing herself within the Birdcage. Her crisis is one of a lack of faith/uncertainty in her abilities, falling into the depths of despair, and hopelessness.
20
The MoonUpright: Illusion, fear, anxiety, insecurity, subconscious

Reversed: Release of fear, unhappiness, confusion		BitchHowling at the moon, hm. Fear, anxiety, a lack of understanding (intentional obfuscation; illusion?) of others, and a reliance on instinct (subconscious) define her and play into her conflicts.
21
The SunUpright: Fun, warmth, success, positivity, vitality

Reversed: Temporary depression, lack of success		BonesawNone more positive or full of vitality than her. Her crisis takes hold in the background, when she finally gets a moment to breathe without Jack Slash around. A quiet, introspective, frustrated journey in a pocket dimension.
22
JudgementUpright: Judgement, rebirth, inner calling, absolution

Reversed: Self doubt, refusal to look inward		LegendThe member of the triumvirate who came back/redeemed himself for his sins, his failure was an inability to recognize what he was doing and what he had gotten himself into. Absolution and a personal calling are definitely applicable.
23
The WorldUpright: Completion, accomplishment

Reversed: Incomplete, lack of closure		ScionThe cycle is his raison d'etre, the breaking of the cycle is his flaw/crisis.
